## Why Kanban Works for Solo Development

### Visual Progress

Human brains process visual information faster than text. A Kanban board shows:
- What's waiting to be done
- What you're currently working on
- What's finished

This visual overview reduces anxiety ("am I forgetting something?") and increases motivation (look how much is in "Done"!).

### Work-In-Progress Limits

Kanban philosophy: limit how many tasks are "in progress" simultaneously.

For solo developers, this is critical. You can't parallelize yourself. Having 15 tasks "in progress" means 15 tasks getting 7% of your attention each.

Easy Kanban Board's visual layout makes WIP limits obvious. If your "In Progress" column is overflowing, you know to finish something before starting something new.

### Flexibility Without Overhead

Kanban doesn't impose:
- Sprint deadlines
- Story point estimation
- Velocity tracking
- Capacity planning

You move tasks when you work on them, and move them again when you finish. That's it.
