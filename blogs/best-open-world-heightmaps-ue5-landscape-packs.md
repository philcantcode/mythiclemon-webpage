## The Open World Terrain Challenge

Creating open world terrain involves:

1. **Learning terrain generation tools** (Gaea, World Creator, etc.)
2. **Designing believable topology** (mountains, valleys, coastlines)
3. **Generating heightmaps** at the right resolution
4. **Importing to Unreal Engine** with correct settings
5. **Creating landscape materials** that paint automatically
6. **Iterating** until it looks right

For a single map, this process takes **40-80 hours**.

For 14 maps? That's a full-time job for months.

## How to Use Heightmap Packs

### Step 1: Choose Your Map

Browse the included maps:
1. Consider your game's setting (islands? mountains? fantasy?)
2. Think about gameplay flow (linear? open? vertical?)
3. Pick the map that fits your vision

### Step 2: Select Resolution

Match resolution to your needs:
- **Prototype phase:** 1K or 2K
- **Production phase:** 4K
- **AAA quality:** 8K

You can always upgrade later.

### Step 3: Import Heightmap

In Unreal Engine:
1. Landscape Mode → Import
2. Select heightmap file
3. Configure scale settings
4. Import

Or use the included project files (already imported and configured).

### Step 4: Apply AutoMaterial

The AutoMaterial textures your landscape automatically:
1. Select landscape
2. Apply M_AutoMaterial instance
3. Terrain instantly textured

### Step 5: Customize (Optional)

Make it your own:
- Paint custom layers over auto-painted areas
- Swap textures in AutoMaterial settings
- Add foliage and vegetation
- Place gameplay elements
